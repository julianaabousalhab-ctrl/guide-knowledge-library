# Give Observers an Active Job When Participation Must Be Sequential

## Pattern

When only one student or pair can perform at a time, assign the remaining students a specific job that requires them to observe, listen, record, encourage, compare, or prepare feedback.

The observer task should be stated before the performance begins and should produce an observable response afterward. Examples include identifying evidence of the target skill, recording one effective move, preparing one question, checking a success criterion, or giving a specific form of encouragement.

Watching quietly is not automatically active participation. An observer has an active job when the student knows what to attend to, why it matters, and what they will do with the information.

## Evidence

*1- Observers analyze performances for specific public-speaking skills*

During sequential pen commercials, the Guide asks the audience to identify evidence of the target criteria in each student’s performance.

> “Can anybody identify where they saw tone, confidence, persuasion, audience engagement, gestures, slogan, things of that nature?”

Students then respond with specific observations:

> “Confidence is like one thing that she looked at us all the time.”

> “Her whole body was engaged.”

This evidence aligns with the pattern because students who are not presenting are responsible for watching for defined public-speaking behaviors. Their observation is then used in the discussion, keeping the audience cognitively involved while participation is sequential.

*2- Observers are assigned a supportive participation job*

During a partner challenge, only one pair performs at a time. Before the first pair begins, the Guide asks the remaining students what they should do while watching.

> “What do you think you should doing while you’re watching?”

> “We can cheer them on.”

> “We can clap for them.”

> “We can uplift them.”

The Guide then reinforces the audience’s participation:

> “I love that you’re cheering. Good job, you guys.”

This evidence aligns with the pattern because waiting students are not left without a purpose. Their assigned job is to provide encouragement that supports the performing pair and reinforces the Launch theme.

## Anti-Pattern

*Directing the audience only to wait quietly*

During a sequence of individual presentations, the Guide calls one student to the front and directs the rest of the class to be quiet.

> “Come on out to the front, please.”

> “Everybody get a chance, guys. Come on.”

> “Hey class, be quiet.”

> “Let’s afford [the presenter] the same opportunity that we gave everyone else, please.”

Protecting the presenter’s opportunity is important, but the audience is given only a behavioral expectation. The transcript does not show an observation target, note-taking task, question to prepare, comparison to make, or feedback responsibility. Students may therefore comply while remaining cognitively passive.

## Reusable Coaching Move

Use a **Watch For–Capture–Contribute** routine:

1. **Watch for:** Name one specific feature before the performance.
   - “Watch for one moment when the speaker uses evidence.”
   - “Listen for the clearest direction the guide gives.”
2. **Capture:** Give observers a simple way to hold their thinking.
   - Write one word, remember one example, or use a checklist.
3. **Contribute:** Require a response after the turn.
   - Share one observation, ask one question, give one piece of specific feedback, or vote with evidence.
4. **Rotate the focus:** Change the observer job between rounds so students attend to different dimensions.
5. **Connect it to their own turn:** Ask, “What will you borrow when it is your turn?”

A practical observer prompt is:

> “While you watch, look for ___. Afterward, be ready to share ___.”

Use supportive observer jobs such as cheering when encouragement is the actual target skill. Use analytical observer jobs when students need to study quality, strategy, communication, or evidence.

Avoid relying only on “watch,” “listen,” or “be quiet.” These are conditions for observation, not complete learning jobs.

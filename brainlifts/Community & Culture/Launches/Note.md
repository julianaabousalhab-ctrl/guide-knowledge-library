# 6.1.1 Growth Mindset — Inclusion Log

## What’s in
- **BL-001:** Build a second attempt into the Launch and require a strategy change.
- **BL-002:** Make mistakes recoverable and keep students in the task.
- **BL-003:** Convert Growth Mindset language into a concrete next move.

## What’s out
Affirmations, slogans, effort praise, competition, or difficulty alone were excluded when they did not lead to a retry, adjustment, recovery, or next action.

## What was ambiguous
Grit, persistence, and Growth Mindset often overlapped. Evidence was included here only when the Guide helped students learn from difficulty, revise an approach, or continue with purpose.

# 6.1.2 Energy & Excitement — Inclusion Log

## What’s in
- **BL-004:** Give the challenge a story, role, problem, or audience.
- **BL-005:** Escalate the challenge in short, visible rounds.

## What’s out
Prizes, XP, music, cheering, countdowns, novelty, and high-energy delivery were not included as standalone patterns because the evidence did not show them reliably deepening participation or learning.

## What was ambiguous
Several examples could also be called task architecture. They remained here because no new node was allowed and their clearest Guide-facing function was to create and sustain purposeful energy.

# 6.1.3 Guide Delivery — Inclusion Log

## What’s in
- **BL-006:** Coach with questions before offering solutions.
- **BL-007:** Model the minimum, then release students to practice.
- **BL-008:** Name the exact behavior or strategy students should repeat.

## What’s out
Generic praise, extended explanation, immediate solution-giving, and enthusiasm without a specific instructional move were excluded as positive patterns.

## What was ambiguous
Modeling and lecturing can look similar. Modeling was treated as positive only when it was brief, clarified the minimum needed, and was followed by student practice.

# 6.1.4 Student Engagement — Inclusion Log

## What’s in
- **BL-009:** Give every student an interdependent role.
- **BL-010:** Give observers an active job when participation must be sequential.

## What’s out
Group work without meaningful role dependency, passive turn-taking, and engagement inferred only from compliance, noise, or excitement were excluded.

## What was ambiguous
Cheering was counted as an active observer job only when encouragement was itself the intended contribution. When the target required thinking, an evidence, question, comparison, or feedback role was treated as stronger.

# 6.1.5 Student Understanding — Inclusion Log

## What’s in
- **BL-011:** Require students to justify answers with evidence.
- **BL-012:** Use contrasting examples and have students infer the distinction.

## What’s out
Choral repetition, yes/no answers, labels without explanation, and Guide-supplied definitions followed only by recall were excluded as evidence of understanding.

## What was ambiguous
Examples and definitions were not treated as weak by default. They counted only when students compared, classified, justified, or applied the distinction themselves.

# 6.1.6 Student Application — Inclusion Log

## What’s in
- **BL-013:** Ask for a specific personal context and action.
- **BL-014:** Embed immediate use in the next authentic task.

## What’s out
Broad transfer questions, Guide-supplied applications, hypothetical contexts without an action, and general reminders to use the skill later were excluded.

## What was ambiguous
The two patterns overlap but were separated by evidence: **BL-013** requires a student-generated context-and-action plan; **BL-014** requires the Guide to structure actual use in the next task.

# 6.1.7 Reflection — Inclusion Log

## What’s in
- **BL-015:** Debrief from evidence, to cause, to next step.
- **BL-016:** Compare approaches and extract a reusable strategy.

## What’s out
Theme-only answers, feelings-only debriefs, winner or score celebrations without analysis, and deferred reflection were excluded.

## What was ambiguous
Next-step reflection can overlap with Growth Mindset and Closing. It stayed here when the Guide was analyzing a completed experience; retries and scheduled continuation were placed in those other nodes.

# 6.1.8 Closing — Inclusion Log

## What’s in
- **BL-017:** Bridge the lesson to the next context with a concrete cue.
- **BL-044:** Preserve continuity by naming when the next attempt will happen.

## What’s out
Generic reminders, abrupt cleanup, vague promises to try again, and transitions without a cue, action, or scheduled continuation were excluded.

## What was ambiguous
Closing overlaps with Student Application. It stayed here when the key Guide move happened at the end of the Launch and either named the next context and cue or scheduled the next attempt.

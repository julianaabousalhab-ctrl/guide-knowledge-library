# Build the Agenda Before the Meeting—and Put a Student Name Beside Every Topic

## Pattern

Create a predictable agenda-submission process before the Limitless Launch, preserve unresolved or recurring student topics, and assign a student owner to every selected agenda item.

The Guide prepares the conditions for ownership without taking over the content. Students know where and when to submit topics, can see what was selected or carried forward, and enter the meeting knowing who will introduce and lead each discussion. During the Launch, the Guide gives the floor to the named student owner rather than presenting the topic on the student’s behalf.

## Evidence

*1- Student submissions were converted into an agenda with a named owner for each item.*

> “The agenda was previously determined based off of students submitting agenda items. So today, [Student A] will be talking about jobs and roles. [Student B] will be doing Indy Minnie and Yet Yeti. [Student A] would like to talk about banning talking about Yet Yeti and Indy Minnie. [Student C] would like to talk about Mars Rovers and [Student C] would like to talk about GEM Kit.”

This shows both parts of the pattern: students submitted the agenda items before the meeting, and specific students were assigned responsibility for leading the topics they brought forward.

Source artifact: [Townhall](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1tk1g9pfceAIAwJzM49EvLOAz3i3f_L3G5ULAke-UhrM/edit?usp=sharing)

*2- The Guide made student authorship visible and returned each topic to its student owner.*

> “You have all previously added these topics to town hall. I was not here when they were added.”

> “There is a yellow card proposal for [Student A]. Remind me who put this on the agenda. [Student B] put that on the agenda, great. Number two, [Student C], how can we help you? This was added by several students. I think there was a total of five students. Who added this. Clarify soccer boundaries. [Student D] added this one. Memes is our carryover from last week from [Student E]. Have they gone too far?”

> “[Student F] has added keeping chargers organized.”

> “So [Student B], go ahead and start off our conversation.”

The Guide did not simply read a list of topics. The Guide identified who submitted each item and explicitly handed the discussion back to the student responsible for it.

Source artifact: [Townhall](https://drive.google.com/file/d/11FZJXEmawIOOaY7uc_mR9ZcsMXV8nRQv/view?usp=sharing)

*3- Follow-up topics were deliberately resubmitted and retained with named student ownership.*

> “We’ve got some follow-up topics that some of our friends have re-added for clarity on next steps. First up, book club. [Student A] has added this. We need book recommendations.”

> “Student newsletter jobs, thank you for bringing that back, [Student B].”

> “Fun Friday. Should Fridays count? Another one added by [Student C].”

This demonstrates a functioning follow-up loop: unresolved topics were not treated as forgotten business, and the students who brought them back remained visibly connected to them.

Source artifact: [Townhall](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1syN9opmukCQ1xpWJdhGKmAPyfQyMdBvSE85Hr2LCzVU/edit?usp=sharing)

*4- Unfinished tickets were preserved across meetings and returned to the student who submitted them.*

> “We had some from last week that we didn’t get to.”

> “All right, so, this is from [Student A]. Do you want to come up here?”

The Townahll retained a backlog instead of discarding unfinished student business. The student author was then invited to take the floor and own the discussion.

Source artifact: [Townhall](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1vTs_ied3tRzxqiFF-52XrVIFZNNdGIDdRTrauhmKecM/edit?usp=sharing)

## Anti-Pattern

The Guide waits until the Townhall begins to solicit topics, or uses an agenda process in which unselected topics disappear without a visible, student-friendly carry-forward system.

This weakens ownership because meeting time is spent inventing topics, students arrive unprepared to lead, and students may stop submitting ideas when they believe their previous submissions were forgotten.

*1- No topics were collected before the meeting, so the Guide had to create an agenda in real time.*

> “I did not receive any topics for discussion for PLayers Parliament this week.”

> “So, if you do, please come up right now, grab a ticket, and grab a pencil, and fill out a ticket really quick for a discussion topic, and then we will start our PLayers’ Parliament.”

The submission process failed before the meeting. Instead of students arriving ready to lead, the Guide paused the Launch and prompted students to generate agenda items on the spot.

Source artifact: [Townhall](https://drive.google.com/file/d/1WYLTar5Skr49zPstKxzG4w4njxZlEpaX/view?usp=sharing)

*2- Students described forgetting to submit and becoming discouraged when unselected topics did not carry forward*

> “Number one is we only submitted two town hall topics this week and they were great town hall topics.”

> “I thought you would remember because I brought out so much time.”

> “If it’s not chosen for a town hall, it, it doesn’t just automatically carry over for the next week. You have to redo it again.”

> “Our topics sometimes aren’t chosen, that’s discouraging.”

The process depended on students remembering to resubmit an idea after it had already been raised. Because the backlog and selection logic were not visible enough to students, worthwhile topics risked being lost and students reported feeling discouraged.

Source artifact: [Townhall](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1npzavA4OjlV7mqGaM7i1Suby-FY77cXOS4Ct_Sfkgl4/edit?usp=drive_link)

## Reusable Coaching Move

Use a **Submit → Curate → Assign → Publish → Carry Forward** routine.

1. **Submit:** Keep one always-available student submission channel, such as a ticket box or digital form. Give students a predictable weekly deadline.
2. **Curate:** Review submissions only for duplication, safety, relevance, or time. Do not rewrite the student’s concern into an adult-owned topic.
3. **Assign:** Put a student owner beside every topic. By default, the submitting student owns the introduction unless the student chooses a peer partner.
4. **Publish:** Before the Launch, display the agenda as:  
   `Topic | Submitted by | Student owner | Decision needed`
5. **Carry Forward:** Automatically preserve unfinished topics in a visible backlog. The student owner decides whether to continue, revise, merge, or withdraw the item.

During the meeting, use one brief handoff:

> “This topic was submitted by [Student A]. [Student A], please explain what the community needs to discuss and what decision you want the group to make.”

The Guide then protects time, access, and respectful process while leaving the topic’s meaning, proposed solutions, and discussion leadership with the student owner.

# Track Guide and System Commitments to Closure

## Pattern
Guides strengthen follow-up coaching when they treat actions owned by the Guide or a broader system as trackable commitments rather than informal promises.

These commitments may include:

contacting an academic or application team;
requesting a test, course, or coaching call;
correcting missing XP;
investigating an application problem;
obtaining a decision from another adult;
or providing a resource the student was promised.

The recurring sequence is:

Name the commitment → record or complete the Guide-owned action → check the external status → update the student → continue following up until Guide-owned action → check the external status → update the student → continue following up until there is a final outcome or an explicit interim resolution

Closure does not always mean the student receives the outcome they requested. A commitment is closed when:

the request is completed;
the system gives a clear final decision;
an alternative is implemented;
or the student receives a transparent status, interim protection, and a defined next action while the external issue remains open.

The Guide should distinguish between:

the Guide’s action: sending the request, documenting evidence, following up, or correcting a temporary record;
and the system’s action: approving, fixing, grading, assigning, or issuing a final decision.

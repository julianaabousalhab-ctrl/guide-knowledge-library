# Node 1.1 — Building Trust

## In

- Validate the student’s perspective before challenging it
- Adjust the Guide’s approach when the student signals a mismatch
- Establish and honor recording boundaries
- Make the purpose and decision rules explicit before asking for buy-in

## Out

- **Protect disclosure or confidentiality beyond recording** — not included because the transcript evidence was not strong or consistent enough to support a separate pattern.
- **Repair relational ruptures explicitly** — not included because the observed behaviors were already covered by validation, adaptive coaching, and repair pathways.
- **Make the agenda transparent and co-owned** — not included separately because the strongest evidence concerned explaining purpose and decision rules rather than jointly setting the full agenda.

## Ambiguous

- **Recording boundaries** was the least certain pattern in this node. Some examples involved simple procedural consent rather than deeper coaching behavior.
- It was retained because the corpus included direct permission, off-record options, and immediate honoring of a pause request.
- Evidence was limited to explicit transcript language. No assumptions were made about privacy policy, access, or confidentiality beyond what the meetings stated.

# Node 1.2 — Getting to Know Students

## In

- Explore the student’s interests until they reveal a usable coaching lever
- Use student knowledge to personalize later coaching

## Out

- **Build every goal around a student interest** — not included because interests should inform coaching only when the connection is authentic.
- **General rapport-building conversation** — not included because friendly conversation alone does not demonstrate that the Guide learned something usable.
- **Remember personal facts about the student** — not included as a separate pattern because remembering matters only when it changes later coaching.

## Ambiguous

- Some interest conversations created strong rapport but did not produce a coaching application.
- These were treated as anti-patterns or incomplete examples rather than evidence of the pattern.
- A coaching lever required a visible connection to a resource, strategy, opportunity, responsibility, or next step.

# Node 1.3 — Student Voice

## In

- Ask for the student’s solution before supplying one
- Preserve student agency when advocating or escalating

## Out

- **Make more space for student thinking** — not included separately because it overlapped with asking for the student’s solution and probing reasoning.
- **Limit Guide monologues** — treated as a quality indicator or anti-pattern, not a distinct coaching pattern.
- **Always require the student to advocate alone** — not included because some situations legitimately require adult authority or escalation.

## Ambiguous

- Guide suggestions were not automatically treated as reducing student voice.
- The deciding factor was sequence: whether the student had a meaningful opportunity to think or speak first.
- Adult escalation was considered agency-preserving when the student remained informed or involved and the Guide added only the support necessary.

# Node 1.4 — Celebration & Recognition

## In

- Recognize the specific behavior or strategy behind progress
- Connect recognition to growth over time

## Out

- **General praise and encouragement** — not included because it does not show the student what produced the result.
- **Celebrate high scores, XP, or completed checks** — not included as a separate pattern unless the Guide connected the outcome to controllable behavior or prior growth.
- **Praise identity or talent** — excluded as evidence because it can reinforce a fixed-trait interpretation.

## Ambiguous

- Some examples contained both specific strategy recognition and growth-over-time recognition.
- They were assigned according to the primary coaching move:
  - naming what produced the result;
  - or comparing the current state with an earlier baseline.
- The same meeting could support both patterns only when it contained clearly distinct statements.

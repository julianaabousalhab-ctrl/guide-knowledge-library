# Write in Reality → Lever → Next Step Order

## Pattern

Write and deliver Guide feedback in three distinct moves:

  - Reality: State what is currently happening using data, frequency, observable behavior, or a student quote.
  - Lever: Identify the strategy, structure, motivation, or missing Guide action influencing the result.
  - Next Step: State the specific action the Guide will take to move the student forward.

This order prevents comments from becoming a collection of observations. It gives the student and parent a clear account of where the student stands, why the outcome is occurring, and what the Guide will do next. 

## Evidence

*1- The Guide identifies inconsistent performance, diagnoses the missing structure, and defines a measurable intervention.*

>“I have not yet built a reliable structure for [Student] to reach 2X in any subject. He meets minimums at times but does not sustain daily effort to push beyond, with writing being the most persistent gap. Focused mornings have occasionally helped, but have not translated into full-week output. I have not yet turned his social motivation and growing independence into an academic system that works. His openness to feedback, maturity in accountability, and willingness to take on independent goals are clear wins. The future goal is to implement a concrete, student-owned plan: use a simple daily XP tracker we build together, review progress every Monday/Wednesday, and require one measurable academic push each week so his social energy begins fueling steady academic results.”

Source artifact: [Session Snapshot](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/13ApdlwIbzC3KLxYnEd3Jdwt9nAVJ5W2itJ2Z46z3KV8/edit?usp=sharing)

*2- The Guide states the student’s weekly results, identifies the condition affecting performance, and names the routine to address it.*

>“[Student] had a wonderful Session 1, earning 100% in Core Skills for six out of the eight weeks. On the weeks she didn’t meet her minimums, we discovered that a lack of sleep impacted her focus and attention during Core Skills time. In Session 2, I’ll work closely with [Student] to help her establish a consistent bedtime routine so she can come to school rested, focused, and ready to perform at her best. With the right balance of rest and structure, [Student] will be able to sustain her strong momentum and continue achieving at a high level each week.”

Source artifact: [Session Snapshot](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1VTubRJLENKro30phfpa-eDtmJGPEbOG7w5-6abeC9bk/edit?usp=drive_link)

*3- The Guide owns an ineffective approach, names the missing lever, and replaces it with personalized actions.*

>“I have not yet shown [Student] how pairing high standards with high support can elevate her confidence and learning experience. My focus has been more on outcomes than on modeling how feedback and structure lead to growth with less frustration. In Session 2, I will guide her through what ‘high standards with high support’ looks like—setting clear goals, creating feedback loops, and celebrating mastery. Using her skateboard brand as a bridge, I’ll help her apply the same creativity and discipline she shows there to her academics, setting her up for continued growth and independence.”

## Anti-Pattern

*Stopping after Reality.*

A comment may accurately describe performance but still leave the student and parent without an explanation of the Guide-controlled lever or a credible next action.

>“[Student] has surpassed her minimum daily XP in every subject and has been consistent in properly tracking her data. She is at or above grade level in every subject other than Writing, where she is still growing. Overall, she is making clear strides academically and building strong learning habits.”

This gives the parent a general status update, but it does not explain what strategy produced the success, what is contributing to the Writing gap, or what the Guide will do next.

## Reusable Coaching Move

Before submitting a Guide Comment, check that a parent can answer these three questions:

  - Reality: What is happening with my child, and what evidence proves it?
  - Lever: What strategy, structure, or Guide action is influencing the result?
  - Next Step: What specifically will the Guide do next, and what change should that produce?

When one answer is missing, revise the comment before sending it.

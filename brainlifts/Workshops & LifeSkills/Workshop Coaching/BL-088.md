# Rehearse Under Test Conditions — Guided Success Does Not Transfer on Its Own

## Pattern

Students who succeed during guided, prompted, low-stakes practice routinely fail the same skill under AlphaTest conditions — prompt-free, timed, individual, in front of an audience. Success in practice conditions does not predict success in assessment conditions; the transfer itself must be rehearsed. The converged practice:

1. **At least one full-conditions rep before AlphaTest day** — independent, prompt-free, timed, same format as the real assessment. This also isolates diagnosis: it separates students who have the skill but need transfer practice from students who need a different intervention.
2. **Progressive pressure ramps** for performance-based assessments: demonstrate to a partner, then a small group, then another guide group, then unfamiliar adults, before the real audience — building performance capacity, not just the skill.
3. **Midpoint checkpoints with feedback**, so preparation quality gets assessed before the final event rather than discovered at it.

The strongest confirmation is an executed contrast: the same students who all failed collaboration AlphaTests under an unrehearsed structure all passed when the workshop provided assigned roles, multiple coached repetitions, and rubric access during practice.

## Anti-Pattern

The anti-pattern is letting the T2P be the first time a student experiences full assessment conditions — treating strong guided practice as sufficient preparation, or letting an important final event carry all the accountability while daily preparation carries none.

*The final event mattered; daily preparation didn't*

> "When the final performance felt important but daily preparation did not, Adriana relied on last-minute effort instead of consistent practice and iteration."

The fix the guide designed — midpoint mini-showcases where "preparation quality receives feedback before the final event" — is the missing rehearsal structure; the year's observed pattern was rising to the occasion instead of building to it, with the Geo Lab arc (Evidence 1) showing where that ends when the occasion is prompt-free and timed.

## Reusable Coaching Move

Use the Map → Ramp → Simulate → Sort process for any workshop ending in a T2P.

### 1. Map the assessment conditions

List what will actually be true on T2P day: alone or team, timed or open, prompted or prompt-free, audience or none. These conditions are part of what must be practiced.

### 2. Ramp the pressure progressively

Schedule reps that add one condition at a time — independence, then time pressure, then audience size — rather than jumping from guided practice to full conditions.

### 3. Simulate fully at least once

Run one complete dress rehearsal under exact test conditions before the real day, with rubric feedback afterward (criteria per BL-088).

### 4. Sort students by what the simulation reveals

Passed guided practice but failed the simulation → transfer reps. Failed both → skill or clarity intervention. Passed both → raise the ceiling. The simulation's diagnostic value is as important as its practice value.

The coaching standard is not:

> "Students demonstrated the skill successfully during practice, so they're ready."

The standard is:

> "Every student has performed the skill at least once under the exact conditions of the assessment — and the guide knows, before T2P day, who transfers and who doesn't."

# BrainLift: Growth Mindset is rarelt inferred - it has to be named

## Pattern

Across Limitless Launches, students successfully completed engaging activities, but only transferred the lesson to Growth Mindset when the guide explicitly named the principle throughout the launch.

## Evidence

In Thanksgiving Entrepreneurship Launch, the Guide framed the challenge as designing something of value that solves someone's problem:

> “There's an Alpha word for this… entrepreneur… someone who sees there's a problem.”

The Student later explained:

> “Because you said they really want Thanksgiving and music and animals.” ([Source artifact](https://drive.google.com/file/d/1PPoU-z9KNanyh-N9d-h9z6AWdhtBbnmZ/view))

In the Listening is Worth a Thousand Mistakes Launch, the guide repeatedly reinforced:

> “Mistakes are okay. Absolutely. They do help you learn, but would you rather listen or make a mistake? Like I said, listening is worth a thousand mistakes.”

Students later reflected:

> “I would skip the videos… and actually, that made it way harder for me. I ended up spending two hours on the tower and I didn't get any XP from it because I didn't watch the video.” ([Source artifact](https://drive.google.com/file/d/14eTjCnch_UNh5LFKc0Ql5NYmSJHf8h6M/view))

## Anti-Pattern

When the activity is engaging but the principle is never named, students often complete the task without connecting it to perseverance, learning, or Stoic values.

In the Self-Love (Valentine) Launch, the theme stayed blurry and not anchored, and while the energy was high it did not translate into cognitive rigor. Asked to name something they like about themselves, students named preferences instead of internal traits, and the guide moved on without correcting it:

> "I love that I love video games" ([Source artifact](https://drive.google.com/drive/folders/1vK57eIOmBaHVTjpGUtLjlIrQb2YRbx20))

## Reusable coaching move

Every 3-5 minutes, restate the principle:

> "This is Growth Mindset because ..."

Don't assume students will make the connection themselves.
